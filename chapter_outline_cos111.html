Creating an Effective Chapter Outline

Understand what you need to know from the chapter

Review the learning objectives at the beginning of the chapter and skim through the information.

Create an outline of the chapter

Use all the headings and subheadings as your topics - but leave small gaps between each to add information as you go along.

Skim for important information

Make notes on the most important content. Look for headings, sub-headings, and terminology that's highlighted or in bold print.

Paraphrase content into your own words

Your notes need to make sense to you. Even if you write your notes in a way that seems like gibberish to others. The notes must make sense to you.

Highlight the important details

Color coding, or highlighting, your notes is a great way to help you remember information.

Set aside time

Set asign time in your schedule to re-read your notes. Consider creating flashcards. This will make it easier to retain the content for exams. The more you go back to your notes, the more familiar you'll become with the information.
